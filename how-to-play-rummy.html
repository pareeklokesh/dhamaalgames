How to play rummy game

Rummy is a card game that is played with a standard set of cards in which the players are required to create melds and runs and go out as soon as they can. It is among the most popular card games all over the world. This game has many variants like Rummy 500, Gin Rummy, Kalooki, Oklahoma rummy, etc. Let's have an overview of this fantastic card game, which has fancied the imagination of cards enthusiasts for centuries now. Typically, a rummy card game can be played by 2 to 6 players, and two decks of cards are used. Each participant has to play with 13 cards in a rummy game. Besides, rummy uses a joker card. The players are expected to draw cards from the stack of cards and discard cards simultaneously. The aim is to make sequences to get rid of the cards.

The online mode of rummy is increasing in popularity with time, and there are lots of websites and apps which enable rummy enthusiasts to play and win from rummy games. There are many websites available in the online mode where one can play rummy for free or for real money. But to play it offline, one needs to have physical cards and a table to play it. But in both the online and offline mode, the methodology of ascertaining the winner remains the same. The rummy rules dictate that the players have to get rid of all of their cards, and the one who can do that the earliest is declared the winner. In eliminating the cards, the players have to score points by forming combinations with the cards that are dealt from a deck.

Besides, there are pure sequences and impure sequences in a rummy game. A pure sequence refers to a group of cards consisting of three or more than three cards in the same suit. Besides, the cards have to be in consecutive order, and although rummy involves the joker, you cannot use the joker or any other wild card to form the sequence in pure sequence. An impure sequence is rather interesting, as it lets one use a joker to create a sequence. An impure sequence can be three or more cards within the same suit, but one can use the joker to form the sequence.

Additionally, rummy also involves the concept of "sets", a conglomeration of three or more than three cards, which have the same face value but belong to different suits. Please remember that you can use the joker or any other predetermined wild card in a set. It is important to remember that a player has to make at least two sequences in a rummy game, out of which at least one of them has to be a pure sequence. Please remember that one can not claim to win any round of a rummy game without a pure sequence.

Deal Rummy

Deal rummy is another one of the most popular variants of the rummy game. Two to six players can play this game. This popular rummy variant requires two packs of cards and two resultant jokers. Play deal rummy, each player is initially given ten cards to deal with, and the remaining cards are placed face down in the middle of the players. Apart from the cards, players are also supplied with an affixed amount of chips to play the game with. The rules mandate that the winner shall get all the other players' chips.

The player who plays from the left of the dealer begins the game. They start by discarding one card, and the next player then must either discard a card or take the card from the discard pile and add it to their hand. The player then discards another card, and the next player either discards a card or takes the card from the discard pile and adds it to their hand. This process continues until all players have run out of cards. The player discards the last card then starts the next round by discarding one card. The winner is the winner who collects all of the cards first (or with the fewest cards if two or more players tie). Since this form of rummy involves chips, the winner of the game is ascertained based on the number of chips one has.
