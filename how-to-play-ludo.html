Frequently Asked Questions

What are the rules for playing ludo?

Ludo is one of the most popular and easiest games to play, and hence, the game is preferred by people of all ages. The game is played with the help of dice and colored pieces. There are no universal rules for playing ludo. However, the following are some of the most common practices that the players need to follow while playing ludo.

1. Players must throw a six-sided dice to determine how far they move their pieces.

2. Players can only move their pieces forwards, and they can only move one space at a time.

3. If a player's piece lands on a space already occupied by another player's piece, then that piece is "captured" and must be returned to the player's home base.

4. Players can opt to block the opponents' pieces to advance by combining two of their own pieces to form a dyad, which can't be crossed by a single piece of any other player.

5. The first player to move all of their pieces to the home base wins the game.

How do you play ludo and win?

Ludo is a simple game that anybody can play without much training or practice.

The simple goal of the ludo game is to race against the opponents and move all four of your pieces around the board and help these pieces/tokens to reach the home slot with the help of a dice. To do this, you must first roll a six. Then, you can move one of your pieces forward or sideways, one space for each number on the dice. If you roll a six again, you can move another piece.

If you want to win in a game of ludo, follow these steps:

1. Get all four of your pieces to the finish line before your opponent. The player who first reaches the home is the winner.

2. Try to block your opponent's pieces so they can't make any more moves. This shall waste their chances more and increase your chance at winning.

3. Force your opponent to forfeit by landing on their pieces multiple times, thereby capturing their pieces, and they shall have to begin from the start, which shall give you a substantial advantage over your opponents.

4. Instead of concentrating all your pieces in a nearby space of a ludo board, try to keep your details as scattered as possible, as this improves your chance of capturing the opponents' pieces, and you shall get more opportunity to finish first.
